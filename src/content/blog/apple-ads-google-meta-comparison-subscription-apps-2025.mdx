iOS subscribers are worth 3.5x more over their lifetime than Android subscribers — yet Android installs cost 60% less to acquire. That single tension defines every budget decision for subscription app developers in 2025, and it's exactly why the Apple Ads vs. Google App Campaigns vs. Meta debate is the most-searched ad network question in mobile growth, according to research from ShyftUp and Watsspace.

This post cuts through the noise with real benchmarks, network-by-network breakdowns, and a recommendation matrix you can act on today.

## Apple Search Ads: The High-Intent Machine

### How It Works

Apple Search Ads (ASA) intercepts users at peak intent — the exact moment they type a keyword into the App Store. With iOS 14.5+ privacy changes decimating cross-app tracking everywhere else, ASA operates inside Apple's own attribution framework, meaning signal quality remains intact even in a cookieless world.

Apple Search Ads Advanced gives you keyword-level bidding, audience refinements (device, age, existing customers), and Search tab + Today tab placements beyond just search results.

### CPI and ROAS Reality

Expect to pay **$2.50–$5.00 CPI** for competitive categories like fitness, productivity, and finance. That sounds steep compared to Meta's floor, but RevenueCat data consistently shows that users acquired through ASA convert to paid subscriptions at **18–26% trial-to-paid rates** — roughly double what Meta delivers.

The math matters: a $4.00 CPI with a 22% trial conversion and a $9.99/month subscription clears payback in under 60 days for most health and wellness apps.

### Best Use Cases for Apple Ads

1. **Keyword-defended moats** — Own your brand terms and competitor brand terms aggressively. CPIs for branded terms can drop to $0.80–$1.50.
2. **High-LTV subscription categories** — Meditation, fitness coaching, language learning, journaling apps where iOS users show strong retention.
3. **Small teams with limited creative bandwidth** — ASA requires zero video assets. Your App Store screenshots do the heavy lifting.
4. **Post-launch visibility spikes** — New apps can use ASA to kickstart keyword ranking signals while organic ASO matures.

### Apple Ads Weaknesses

- Limited reach at the top of funnel (demand capture, not demand creation)
- Audience expansion is restricted compared to Meta's lookalike engine
- CPIs spike fast in competitive verticals during Q4

## Google App Campaigns: The Volume Engine

### How It Works

Google App Campaigns (GAC) automates placement across Search, Play Store, YouTube, Discover, and the Display Network from a single campaign. You feed it assets — headlines, descriptions, images, videos — and Google's ML optimizes delivery toward your tCPI or tCOA (target cost per action).

For Android-first apps, GAC is essentially non-optional at scale. The Play Store search experience mirrors App Store intent, and YouTube pre-rolls capture mid-funnel audiences that don't exist on Apple's network.

### CPI and ROAS Reality

Android CPI on GAC runs **$0.50–$1.80** — as low as one-fifth the cost of an equivalent iOS install on ASA. But the LTV gap is real. According to RevenueCat's 2024 subscription data, Android subscribers churn approximately 35% faster in the first 90 days compared to iOS cohorts in the same app category.

The 30-day ROAS on GAC typically lands at **0.25–0.45x**, meaning you're investing heavily upfront against longer payback windows. This math only works if your subscription price point is $10+/month or you have strong annual plan conversion.

### Best Use Cases for Google App Campaigns

1. **Android-first or cross-platform apps** — If you're not on Android, you're leaving 70%+ of global smartphone users on the table.
2. **High-volume, lower-ARPU subscription models** — Freemium apps with large user bases that monetize through ads + subscriptions benefit from GAC's scale.
3. **Global expansion plays** — Southeast Asia, Latin America, and Eastern Europe skew heavily Android. GAC is your only meaningful paid channel in these regions.
4. **Video-forward brands** — If you have YouTube content or strong video assets, GAC's YouTube placement is uniquely powerful for storytelling.

### Google App Campaigns Weaknesses

- Black-box optimization with limited transparency into which placements convert
- Slower signal learning compared to Meta (needs 50+ conversions/week to exit learning phase)
- Android LTV deficit requires longer payback tolerance

## Meta (Facebook/Instagram): The Creative Demand Generator

### How It Works

Meta's App Campaign objective targets users across Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and the Audience Network. Unlike ASA or GAC, Meta creates demand rather than capturing it — your ad introduces the app to someone who wasn't searching for it. This makes creative quality the #1 performance variable, full stop.

Meta's Advantage+ App Campaigns (launched 2023, iterated through 2024) have reduced manual targeting in favor of automated audience expansion, leaning heavily on Meta's aggregate signal pools post-ATT.

### CPI and ROAS Reality

Meta CPIs cluster at **$1.50–$4.00** on iOS and **$0.60–$2.00** on Android — competitive with GAC but with significantly more variance driven by creative fatigue. Campaigns that aren't refreshed every 10–14 days see CPIs climb 40–60% as frequency rises.

Trial-to-paid CVR on Meta-acquired users sits at **8–14%** — the lowest of the three networks. The audience quality gap versus ASA is real, but Meta partially compensates with volume and audience scale that neither Apple nor Google can match.

ROAS at 30 days averages **0.20–0.40x**, but Meta's real payoff is in the **6–12 month cohort** — particularly for lifestyle and entertainment apps where brand recall compounds over time.

### Best Use Cases for Meta

1. **Top-of-funnel audience building** — Seed remarketing lists and lookalike audiences that feed your ASA and GAC campaigns downstream.
2. **Creative testing at scale** — Meta's split testing infrastructure is unmatched. Run 10 creative variants simultaneously to identify winning hooks before committing budget elsewhere.
3. **Impulse-friendly subscription categories** — Recipe apps, dating apps, casual games with subscriptions — categories where emotional creative drives spontaneous trials.
4. **Retargeting lapsed users** — Custom audiences of churned subscribers can be re-engaged at 60–70% lower CPA than cold acquisition.

### Meta Weaknesses

- ATT opt-out rates (~75% on iOS) significantly degrade signal quality and raise effective CPIs
- Creative production demand is relentless; small teams burn out fast
- Trial conversion rates are structurally lower than intent-based channels

## The 2025 Verdict

There's no single winner — but there is a smart sequencing strategy:

**Start with Apple Search Ads.** Brand and competitor keywords give you the highest-intent users at manageable CPIs with zero creative investment. This validates your subscription conversion funnel before you scale.

**Layer in Google App Campaigns** once you've confirmed LTV and can tolerate Android's longer payback window. If your app is cross-platform or you're targeting emerging markets, GAC isn't optional.

**Add Meta last**, and treat it as a creative R&D lab and demand-generation engine rather than a direct-response channel. Use it to build retargeting audiences and test hooks — then let those learnings inform your ASA and GAC messaging.

The developers who outperform in 2025 aren't picking one network and going all-in. They're running a coordinated three-channel system where each network does what it's structurally built to do.
